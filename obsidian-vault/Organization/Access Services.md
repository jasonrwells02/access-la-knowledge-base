# Access Services (ASI)

**Consolidated Transportation Services Agency (CTSA)** for Los Angeles County

Administers ADA complementary paratransit services for 46 fixed-route transit operators in Los Angeles County. Provides curb-to-curb, shared-ride transportation for people with disabilities who cannot use fixed-route public transit.

## Key Stats
| Metric | Value |
|---|---|
| Service Area | 1,950 square miles |
| Registered Riders | 115,000 |
| Annual Trips | 3,400,000 |
| Member Agencies | 44 |
| Service Regions | 6 |
| Fixed-Route Operators | 46 |

## Service Coverage
Within 3/4 mile of fixed-route bus lines and METRO Rail stations in Los Angeles County. Extends into portions of San Bernardino, Orange, and Ventura counties served by LA County bus lines.

## Service Hours
- Regular: 4:00 AM to 12:00 AM daily
- Limited: 12:00 AM to 4:00 AM (limited service)

## Funding
- **Proposition C**: Sales tax (0.5% discretionary funds)
- **Federal Section 5310 grants**: Federal grant
- **Fare-box revenue**: Operating revenue

## Fleet
- Vehicle types: Small buses, Mini-vans, Taxis
- Fleet leased to regional providers at $1/month

## CTSA Functions
- Information and technical assistance to LA County specialized transportation providers
- Learning and Development Program for transit professionals
- Legislative collaboration with CalACT
- Participation in Metro LTSS and BOS planning meetings
- Technical assistance for Section 5310, 5316 (JARC), and 5317 (New Freedom) grants
- Coordinated Action Plan development for SCAG

## Regulatory Compliance
- ADA (Americans with Disabilities Act of 1990)
- Title VI (Civil Rights Act of 1964)
- Section 504 (Vocational Rehabilitation Act of 1973)
- Civil Rights Restoration Act of 1987
