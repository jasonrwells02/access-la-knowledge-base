# FAQs: Programs

## What is Access to Work?
Access to Work is a standing order service for riders commuting to employment or job training. Trips are scheduled based on your work start time so you arrive on time. If your hours change, you can modify trip times without reapplying. You need a consistent weekly schedule to qualify. Contact 213-270-6000 for more information.

## What is Beyond the Curb?
Beyond the Curb provides extra assistance where the driver accompanies you up to 60 feet from the vehicle to your destination. It's available as a reasonable modification for riders who can't navigate from the curb on their own. Request it when you make your reservation, or apply through the Reasonable Modification form by calling 800-827-0829, Option 6.

## What is the Free Fare Program?
All certified Access riders can ride most fixed-route buses and trains in LA County for free using their green Access Rider ID Card as a TAP card. No registration needed. It covers 24 transit operators including Metro Bus, Metro Rail, Long Beach Transit, Big Blue Bus, and more. It does not affect your paratransit eligibility.

## What is the Parents with Disabilities Program?
This program helps Access-certified parents or guardians take children age 16 and under to and from school and school activities. Trips are scheduled around school times so your child arrives on time. The fare is $2.00 one way. You can get linked trips to continue to another destination after drop-off. Contact Eric Haack at 213-270-6000.

## What is Travel Training?
Travel Training is free one-on-one training that teaches you how to safely use fixed-route public transit like buses and trains. You learn schedule reading, fare payment, boarding, transfers, and safety skills. It's provided by Mobility Management Partners at 888-667-7003. Participation doesn't affect your Access eligibility.

## What is a reasonable modification?
A reasonable modification is a change to Access policies or practices to accommodate your disability so you can use the paratransit system. For example, Beyond the Curb service. To request one, call 800-827-0829 press #6, or contact the Reasonable Modification Coordinator Torrance Johnson at 213-270-6159 or RMC@accessla.org.
